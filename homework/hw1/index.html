Amount of Trees Planted Across the Years

Within our choropleth map, we saw that only a few SF neighborhoods had average tree ages above 31+ years. I wanted to dive into this further by examining the amount of tree planting initiatives across the decades. Looking at our line graph, we can see there is a huge boom in tree planting from 2005-2010 (which lines up with our map), and a huge drop after 2020. Older trees tend to be more draught resistant than younger ones, and more efficient with CO2 absorption. This suggests that the SF community should begin replanting efforts soon, in the hopes of building an overall more resilient community in the future when climate change exacerbates weather conditions.
